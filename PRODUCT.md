# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Local business owners: salons, restaurants, bike shops, auto shops, clinics, med spas, dentists, gyms, repair shops, retail. They rely on inbound calls for revenue but are not technical. They check results after the fact, not in real time. They care about money, not metrics.

## Product Purpose

Scout.ai calls your business like a real customer, scores the conversation, and shows exactly which sentences lost the sale. It exists to turn invisible call quality into a concrete, actionable coaching report. Success is an owner who walks away knowing what to fix and how to fix it.

## Brand Personality

Warm, coaching, direct. Feels like a trusted business coach — encouraging, human, and built for non-technical owners. Not a developer tool. Not a cold analytics dashboard. More like a performance review from someone who's on your side.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream (white backgrounds, teal accents, Intercom-style startup aesthetic)
- Hacker/terminal aesthetic (monospace fonts, neon-on-black, CLI energy)
- Enterprise bloat (heavy sidebars, nested navbars, Salesforce-style information overload)

## Design Principles

1. **Show the money** — every screen should make the financial stakes legible. Score + revenue impact first, details second.
2. **Coach, don't judge** — framing is always constructive. Even a bad score feels like feedback, not a verdict.
3. **Owner-first clarity** — no jargon, no developer abstractions. Language a restaurant owner would use themselves.
4. **Signal over chrome** — the data is the product. Decoration should never compete with findings.
5. **Action at every step** — after a report, the owner should always know what to do next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Assume color-blind users; never rely on color alone to convey score severity. Reduced motion should be respected. Mobile-comfortable (owners may check reports on their phone).
