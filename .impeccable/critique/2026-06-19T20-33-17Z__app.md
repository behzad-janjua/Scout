### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: Yes, this looks AI-generated. Four specific tells:

1. The palette (`#0b0f14` + `#4f9dff` + `#28e0a8`) is the exact color trio in ~40% of AI-generated dark dashboards. It reads as "developer tool midnight mode."
2. The `.card` pattern is applied to every element — 12+ cards across homepage, create, and report pages, all with identical 20px padding, 12px radius, same border. No element breaks the grid. Every section has identical visual weight.
3. The `.panel-label` (uppercase, muted, 0.78rem) appears 8+ times identically. It is a visual tic.
4. The homepage's two-column grid with bullet lists ("How it works" / "Powered by") is one of the most common AI-generated homepage patterns.

The most damaging tell is tonal: **the stated brand is "warm, coaching, direct" but the aesthetic is cold, monitoring-dashboard, developer-tool.** A salon owner logging in would feel they've opened the wrong app.

### Overall Impression

Scout.ai has a clear product concept and a working technical scaffold. The problem is it was built in "developer tool" mode while the users are local business owners. Every decision — midnight-black background, ALL_CAPS panel labels, terminal-adjacent color palette — signals "monitoring dashboard for engineers." The single biggest opportunity is a tonal shift: warmer, lighter, more human. The product is about people and money, and the UI should feel that way.

### What's Working

1. **The create flow is clean and fast.** Two forms side by side, clear CTA, live status updates. The async polling pattern with status messages is the right approach for a call-based product.

2. **The score badge communicates quickly.** The circular score badge with color-coded categories gives the report its emotional anchor. The `score_category` → color mapping is intuitive even without reading the legend.

3. **The report data model is rich.** `worst_sentences` with `exact_sentence`, `issue`, `why_it_hurt_conversion`, and `better_response` is genuinely useful coaching data. The structure is well-thought-out even if the presentation undersells it.

### Priority Issues

**[P1] Tonal mismatch — the interface looks like a dev tool, not a coaching product**
- **What**: Dark, cold, terminal-adjacent aesthetic contradicts the "warm, coaching, direct" brand personality. The product targets salon owners and restaurant operators, not SREs.
- **Why it matters**: Non-technical business owners won't trust an interface that looks like a hacker dashboard. First impressions decide whether they complete a test or close the tab.
- **Fix**: Write the scene sentence: "A restaurant owner checking her staff's call score at 9am before opening." That sentence does not resolve to dark mode on a midnight background. Move to a warm light theme, or substantially increase warmth and contrast in the current dark palette. The color strategy should be Restrained-warm, not Restrained-cold.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable bolder` or `/impeccable colorize`

**[P1] Side-stripe border in WorstSentencesPanel (absolute ban)**
- **What**: `WorstSentencesPanel.tsx:20` — `borderLeft: "3px solid var(--danger)"` with `paddingLeft: 12` on each worst-sentence item.
- **Why it matters**: A red accusation stripe per sentence makes coaching feel like a verdict. It reinforces criticism over improvement, which is the opposite of the brand.
- **Fix**: Replace with a full-background danger tint, a leading warning icon, or a numbered sequence. The sentence should feel like something to learn from, not a flagged error.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish`

**[P1] No confirmation before placing a live call**
- **What**: "Start mystery call" fires immediately with no confirmation, no warning that a real call will be placed to the listed business number, no cost notice.
- **Why it matters**: This is an irreversible action. First-timers won't know a real phone call is happening. If the number is wrong, there is no undo.
- **Fix**: Pre-call confirmation: show the phone number being called, the scenario title, and a "Yes, place the call" button. At minimum, a label near the CTA: "This will call [phone number] — calls are real and billed."
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden`

**[P2] Report has no narrative — 7 equal sections with no "so what"**
- **What**: The report renders 7 sections with identical visual weight: summary card, call card, transcript, category scores, checklist, revenue moments, worst sentences, recommended script. No section is more prominent than another.
- **Why it matters**: A business owner doesn't have time to read 7 sections. They want: score + main issue + one fix. That narrative doesn't surface.
- **Fix**: Lead with a one-screen verdict (score + one-sentence finding + one action). Make the remaining sections progressive disclosure. Elevate RecommendedScript visually — it's the payoff of the whole report.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Error recovery in the create flow is a dead end**
- **What**: `create/page.tsx:57` — errors set `status` to `Error: ${(err as Error).message}`. No retry button, no diagnosis, no recovery path. The user is stranded with a muted string.
- **Why it matters**: Call failures are common (Vapi timeouts, webhook delays). A first user whose test fails sees a technical error message and has no guidance.
- **Fix**: On error, render an inline error card with plain-language reason, a "Try again" button that re-runs the same scenario, and a way to contact support.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden`

### Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer — a curious restaurant owner trying Scout for the first time):**
- Lands on homepage. "Powered by Vapi · Nebius · Insforge" on the second card means nothing to her. This reads as corporate name-dropping.
- Creates a test. The phone number field has no label indicating this is the number that will be called. She may enter her personal number by mistake.
- Clicks "Start mystery call" with no confirmation. A real call is placed to her business. She doesn't know this.
- Status updates: "Current status: in_progress." She reads this as a technical glitch, not a status update.
- If it works: she must scroll through 7 equal-weight sections to understand the result. No summary verdict at the top.
- If it fails: muted error string. No guidance. She closes the tab.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile Owner — checking a report between customers):**
- The `grid-2` correctly stacks on mobile. But the `spread` layout (score badge + back link + h1) is cramped at mobile width — three elements sharing one row.
- The `row` layout in CallSummaryCard (`flexWrap: wrap, gap: 24`) creates orphaned stat labels at intermediate viewport widths.
- The recording link is a plain `<a>` tag with an arrow prefix — a tiny tap target.
- `WorstSentencesPanel` renders 4 text blocks per sentence × 3 sentences = 12 consecutive text runs with no visual anchoring on mobile.
- `RecommendedScript` at the bottom is the key payoff, but on mobile she may never reach it through the scrollable report.

**Marcus (Non-Technical Local Owner) — project-specific persona:**
Profile: bike shop owner, iPhone user, checks things between customers, moderately tech-comfortable.
- "LEAD CAPTURE CHECKLIST" — he doesn't use "lead capture" in his vocabulary. He knows if someone called about a repair and he booked them.
- "Customer intent: High" — "intent" reads as a legal or marketing term, not plain speech.
- "Worst sentences" — the heading frames this as an accusation against his employee, not a coaching tool.
- He can't share the report. There's no share link, export, or email option. His manager on the floor can't access this coaching data.
- After reading the recommended script, he thinks "great, now what?" There's no copy button, no print, no forward. The coaching insight is trapped in the browser tab.

### Minor Observations

- `.brand-dot` (glowing green circle in the header) is not animated — either animate it with a subtle pulse or drop it. A static dot implies status it doesn't convey.
- `font-size: 0.78rem` on `.panel-label` is ~12.5px at default browser size. On small viewports, this may fall below WCAG AA minimum for non-decorative text.
- Homepage footer: "AI secret shopping for phone calls · Vapi · Nebius · Insforge" is developer attribution, not user-facing copy.
- Transcript speaker labels both use `var(--accent)` (same blue for Scout.ai and the employee). No visual differentiation between speakers in the conversation.
- A `demo/` directory exists but is not linked in the nav. If this is a demo mode, it should be discoverable.
- No favicon — the browser tab shows the Next.js default icon.
